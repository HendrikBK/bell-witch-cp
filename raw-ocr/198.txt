The crisis, however, came on the mornirg of
December 19th. Father, sick as he was, bad not
up to this time failed to awake at his regular
hour, according to his long custom, and arouse
the family. That morning he appeared to be
sleeping so soundly, mother quietly slipped out
of the room to superintend breakfast, while
brothers John and Drew looked after the farm
hands and feeding the stock, and would not allow,
him to be disturbed until after breakfast. Notic-
ing then that he was sleeping unnaturally, it was
thought best to awaken him, when it was discov-
ered that he was in a deep stupor, and could not
be aroused to any sensibility. Brother John
attended to giving him medicine, and went im-
mediately to the cupboard where he had carefully
put away the medicines prescribed for him, but
instead he found a smoky looking vial, which
was about one-third full of dark colored liquid.
He set up an inquiry at once to know who had
moved the medicine, and no one had touched it,

account of the vial. Dr. George Hopson, of Port
