Mrs. Kate Batts was the wife of Frederick Batts,
who was terribly afflicted, and she had become
the head of the family, taking charge of her hus-

sative. Some people were disposed to shun her,
which was still more irritating to her sensative
nature. No harm could he said of Mrs. Batts.
She was kind hearted, and a good neighbor toward
those she liked. Mr. Gunn, of course, did not
believe the witch’s statement, but many did, or
professed to, and the matter made Mrs. Batts very
mad, causing a lively sensation in the community.
