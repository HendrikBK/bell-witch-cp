Being assured that there was not much danger,
that Kate would be too busy entertaining the
company to take any notice of her, her fears gave
way to her plucked up courage and she followed
mother’s directions to the letter, keeping the whole
matter a secret from the other negroes and all the
family until the test was made as to whether the
witch would trouble ber or not. So one evening
after supper Anky quietly slipped in the room
with her pallet and spread it under mother’s bed,
fixing herself comfortly on it, to await the coming
in of visitors and the witch and hear the talk-
ing. It was a high bedstead, with a white
fringed, counterpane hanging to the floor, hiding
Anky completely. She was delighted, and net a
soul except mother knew she was there. Very
soon the room was filled with visitors, keeping
