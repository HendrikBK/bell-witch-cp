Mr. Wiliams was rather a portly, strong-muscled,
well dressed, handsome gentleman. He was no
less self-possessed, and wise in his own conceit,
full of gab, letting his tongue run continually,
detailing to the company his wonderful exploits
in the detective business, and was very sure he
would bring Kate to grief before leaving. A day
and night passed and Kate, for some cause best
known to the witch, kept silent, making no show

ing about the room, just enough to let the com-
pany know that the spirit was present. Mr.
