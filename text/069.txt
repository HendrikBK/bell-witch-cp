CHAPTER VI.

Mrs, Kate Batts and the Witch.

It is proper that the reader should, before per-
using “Our Family Trouble” and other accounts
of the witch, be introduced to Mrs. Kate Batts,
who was a noted lady in that community, re-
markable for her eccentricities, who survived long
after John Bell, and is well remembered by many
citizens still living. There were two Batts fami-
lies, who were in no way related. Jerry Batts
was a very prominet man, and his descendants
make up part of the present good citizenship of
that community.

Frederick Batts and wife Kate had three child-
ren, Jack, Calvin and Mary. They had no rela-
tives and lived very much unto themselves.
