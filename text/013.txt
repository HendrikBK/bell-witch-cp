James Allen Bell, who has carefully preserved it.
The writer was raised within a few miles of the
Bell place, and has been familiar with the witch
story from his youth up, and becoming intimately
acquainted with Joel and Allen Bell during his
residence in Springfield, about 1867 applied to
Joel Bell for the privilege of writing the history
then, while himself, sister Betsy, Frank Miles,
Lawson Fort, Patrick McGowen, Johnson, and
others acquainted with the facts, were still living.
Joel Bell assented to the proposition, but Allen
Bell declined to furnish his father’s manuscript,
and the matter was dropped until recently.
