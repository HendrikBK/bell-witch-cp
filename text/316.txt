Mrs. Ayers, did you ever hear anything detri-
mental in any way to the character of Joun Bell
or his family? “Not a breath in the world. No
man or family stood higher in the estimation of
the people than John Bell. I have heard him
spoken of as one of the leading men of the coun-
try, and father said the citizens had the utmost
confidence in his integrity. More than that, he
raised his children to be honorable men and
women, and the family influence is felt in Rob-
ertson county to this day; even the grandchildren
are men of the same substantial character.”

Do you remember anything about Rey. Thomas
and Rev. James Gunn? “Yes, certainly; they
were the founders of Methodism in this section,
and Rev. Sugg Fort was the leading Baptist.
Their lives were full of good works and honors.
I have heard it said many times that they visited
Mr. Bell often and sympathized with him in his
distress.”
